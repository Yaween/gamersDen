ABOUT ESPORTS

Video game competitions are known as esports, or electronic sports. Esports frequently take the shape of planned, competitive multiplayer video games, especially involving professional players, either individually or in teams. While though organized tournaments have always been a part of the video gaming culture, these were primarily amateur contests up until the late 2000s, when professional gamers began to participate and live streaming of these events experienced a significant increase in popularity. Esports has become a substantial part of the video game industry by the 2010s, with many game developers actively creating and financing tournaments and other events.

VALORANT

First-person tactical hero shooter Valorant is a free-to-play Windows game created and released by Riot Games. The game was teased in October 2019 under the pseudonym Project A, then on April 7, 2020, a closed beta with restricted access started. On June 2, 2020, the game was officially released. The tactical shooter series Counter-Strike serves as an inspiration for Valorant, which borrows various gameplay elements from it.

CS:GO

A multiplayer tactical first-person shooter called Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO) was created by Valve and Hidden Path Studios in 2012. The fourth title in the Counter-Strike series is this one. Global Offensive, a game that took over two years to create, was launched in August 2012 for OS X, PlayStation 3, Windows, and Xbox 360. It was later released for Linux in 2014.

APEX

Developed by Respawn Entertainment and released by Electronic Arts, Apex Legends is a free-to-play battle royale-hero shooting game. It was released in February 2019 for PlayStation 4, Windows, and Xbox One, in March 2021 for Nintendo Switch, and in March 2022 for PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S.
